The Problem

Why Universities
Are Broken.

Students at most universities operate across 8–12 disconnected systems — a separate portal for registrar, another for dining, a third for library, different apps for transport and health. No single system understands intent. No system can answer "what do I do next?"

Admin teams receive the same questions hundreds of times per week. Tier-1 support is dominated by queries like "where do I drop a course?" and "how do I request a transcript?" — tasks a well-designed AI system could handle in seconds.

Core Insight

The problem isn't missing information — it's missing intelligence. Every answer exists somewhere on the university website. The gap is that students can't find it when they need it, in the way they think about it.

Users & Pain Points

  • Students: Navigate fragmented systems; can't find answers across silos; high cognitive load during critical decisions (registration, graduation, aid)
  • Admin Teams: Overwhelmed by repetitive tier-1 queries; no visibility into what students are struggling with at scale
  • Service Departments: Data exists in isolation; no unified way to surface it contextually
  • University Leadership: No product-level intelligence on student friction points; decisions made from lagging indicators
PM Thinking

How I Approached
This Problem.

Customer Wedge

Don't boil the ocean. The entry wedge is first-year student orientation — the highest-anxiety, highest-friction moment in the university lifecycle. Students need to navigate housing, registration, dining, ID, email, and financial aid simultaneously. Nailing this moment builds trust and stickiness for four years.

Personas

  • The Overwhelmed Freshman (Sam): First-gen student, 18, never used a university portal before. Primary goal: not fall behind. Won't read FAQs. Will talk to a chat interface at 11pm.
  • The Power User (Jamie): Senior, double major, knows the system. Uses the tool for edge cases: petitions, appeals, unofficial transcript timelines, research lab applications.
  • The Admin Coordinator (Dr. Chen): Processes 60+ student requests/week manually. Wants escalation clarity — know when to step in, have AI handle the rest.

Prioritization Framework

Applied RICE scoring across 18 proposed features. Top three by RICE: (1) Registrar Q&A — highest reach, daily frequency; (2) AI Copilot — highest impact, core product belief; (3) Admin Dashboard — unlocks institutional adoption. Deferred: gamification, social features, calendar sync (high effort, low retention evidence).

MVP Scope Decision

What's In MVP

Student command center with RAG copilot · Registrar + library + dining + transport Q&A · Citation-based answers with confidence thresholds · Admin escalation queue · Single-department pilot (Registrar). What's NOT in MVP: multi-institution support, mobile native app, real-time inventory.

Groundedness Score
  • North Star: Student sessions ending with "I got my answer" (self-reported or inferred via no-escalation)
  • Engagement: WAU, queries per session, repeat usage by cohort
  • Quality: Hallucination rate, citation accuracy, escalation precision, time-to-resolve in admin queue
  • Trust: NPS, admin override rate (leading indicator of AI trust), escalation abuse rate

Experiment Backlog (Priority Order)

  • A/B: Citation format — footnote-style vs. inline link. Hypothesis: inline improves confidence without slowing reading.
  • A/B: Confidence threshold — Surface answer at 70% vs 80% groundedness. Tradeoff: coverage vs accuracy.
  • Test: Persona-adaptive tone — Does adjusting formality by student year improve NPS?
  • Test: Proactive nudges — Push alerts during registration windows. Hypothesis: reduces deadline-driven surge queries.

Risks & Tradeoffs

  • Hallucination risk: Mitigation — citation-only answers, hard fallback to escalation at <70% confidence
  • Data freshness: University policies change mid-semester. Mitigation — source TTL tagging, re-ingestion triggers on CMS edits
  • Institutional adoption: IT security review, FERPA compliance, SSO integration are pre-GTM gates, not afterthoughts
  • Cold-start quality: RAG accuracy is dataset-dependent. Mitigation — curate and validate 200+ QA pairs before launch
AI Product Depth

RAG Architecture
& Eval Framework.

Campus Atlas is built on Retrieval-Augmented Generation — not a fine-tuned model, not prompt engineering alone. RAG was chosen because university data changes frequently (policies, deadlines, personnel), and we need traceability to source documents for FERPA-defensible answers.

Evaluation Framework

  • Groundedness score: Cross-reference answer against retrieved chunks. Target ≥ 0.80. Computed via LLM-as-judge + cosine similarity.
  • Citation accuracy: Does the cited source URL actually contain the claim? Manual spot-check 10% of production answers weekly.
  • Hallucination rate: Answers that contain information absent from retrieved context. Target < 3%. Tracked via eval harness on synthetic test set.
  • Escalation precision: Are the escalated queries the ones that needed human review? Measure: admin override rate on AI-answered queries.
  • Human-in-the-loop: Admin sees escalated query + reason for escalation + relevant source chunks. Can approve, correct, or override. Corrections feed back into eval log.

Data Ingestion Strategy

  • Source: University website, course catalog PDF, registrar policy docs, dining menus, campus maps
  • Chunking: 512-token chunks with 64-token overlap; section-aware splitting for policy docs
  • Metadata: source URL, document type, last-updated timestamp, department, TTL
  • Refresh: Nightly re-ingestion for high-churn sources (dining, library hours); policy docs on CMS trigger

GTM Strategy

  • Landing customer: Target mid-size universities (5,000–20,000 students) with active digital transformation initiatives. Avoid Tier-1 research universities first (long procurement cycles).
  • Champion: VP of Student Affairs or Dean of Students — they own the pain, have budget discretion, and are frustrated by IT-led portals that don't solve student problems.
  • Sales motion: Pilot with one department, measure deflection, show ROI in admin hours saved. Expand to university-wide on proven metrics.
  • Pricing: Per-institution SaaS, tiered by student enrollment. Freemium single-department pilot. Full contract at university level.
  • Launch moment: New student orientation — maximum anxiety, maximum query volume, maximum proof of value.
